About Shark Feeding Types

Filter Feeders

Filter feeding sharks swim with their mouths open, using specialized gill rakers to strain tiny organisms from the water.

Examples: Whale Shark, Basking Shark, Megamouth Shark

Diet: Plankton, krill, small fish, algae

Non-Filter Feeders

Most sharks are active predators with powerful jaws and teeth designed for capturing and consuming larger prey.

Examples: Great White Shark, Hammerhead Shark, Thresher Shark

Diet: Fish, seals, squid, other sharks

Key Differences

Filter Feeders: Eat tiny organisms, have modified gill rakers, generally harmless to humans

Non-Filter Feeders: Hunt larger prey, have rows of sharp teeth, use various hunting strategies
